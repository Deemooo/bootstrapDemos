Turing is widely considered to be the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

King's College, Cambridge, where Turing was a student in 1931 and became a Fellow in 1935. The computer room is named after him.

The timeline of alan turing's life

  • 1912 (23 June): Birth, Paddington, London
  • 1926-31: Enrolls in the Sherborne School
  • 1930: His friend Christopher Morcom dies
  • 1931-34: Undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge University, studied Quantum mechanics, probability, logic.
  • 1936: Invents the idea for the Turing Machine
  • 1936-38:  Attends Princeton University and receives his Ph.D. in logic, algebra, and number theory
  • 1939: Alan Turing starts work at Bletchley Park
  • 1939-42: Turing and his team break the U-boat Enigma, saving Battle of the Atlantic
  • 1945-48: Works at National Physics Laboratory
  • 1950: The Turing Test for machine intelligence is published in the journal “Mind”
  • 1952: Is arrested as a homosexual, loses security clearance
  • 1954 (7 June): Death (suicide) by cyanide poisoning, Wilmslow, Cheshire. He ate an apple laced with cyanide.

To mark the 100th anniversary of Turing's birth, the Turing Centenary Advisory Committee (TCAC) co-ordinated the Alan Turing Year, a year-longprograme of events around the worldhonoringg Turing's life and achievements. The TCAC, chaired by S. Barry Cooper with Alan Turing's nephew Sir John Dermot Turing acting as Honorary President, worked with the University of Manchester faculty members and a broad spectrum of people from Cambridge University and Bletchley Park.
